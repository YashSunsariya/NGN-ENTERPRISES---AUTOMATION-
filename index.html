Solar Panels — Detailed Product Theory

What are Solar Panels? ▼

What is a solar panel?

A solar panel (also called a photovoltaic or PV module) is an assembly of silicon solar cells that convert sunlight into direct current (DC) electricity. Cells are connected in series and parallel inside a durable frame with tempered glass and an encapsulant to protect from weather.

How does a panel work (short technical)

When photons strike the silicon cells, they excite electrons and set up a flow (current). Series-connected cells increase voltage and parallel strings increase current. The module's electrical output depends on irradiance (W/m²), temperature, and cell efficiency.

Where are panels used?

Rooftop residential systems, commercial rooftop and canopy installations, ground-mounted solar farms, solar pumps for agriculture, street lighting, remote telecom sites and off-grid standalone systems.

Types of solar panels

  • Monocrystalline (Mono) — Single-crystal silicon, high efficiency (18–22%+), darker appearance. Best for limited roof area.
  • Polycrystalline (Poly) — Lower cost, slightly lower efficiency, blueish appearance.
  • Thin-film — Lightweight, flexible, lower efficiency — used where weight or shape is critical.
  • Bifacial — Captures light on both sides when installed with reflective ground or elevated mounting.
  • TOPCon / PERC / HJT — Advanced cells with better efficiency and temperature performance used in modern commercial modules.
Panel Installation Guidance (step-by-step) ▼
  1. Site survey: Record roof area, azimuth, tilt, obstructions, shadow profiles across seasons. Assess cable routes and inverter location. Take photos and measurements to create a layout drawing.
  2. Structural check: Confirm roof can take additional load. Consult civil/structural if needed; use anchor points suitable for material (tile, metal, concrete).
  3. Mounting structure & tilt: Choose fixed-tilt rails or adjustable racks. Calculate wind load, torque specs, and use corrosion-resistant fasteners.
  4. Panel placement: Place panels in strings; ensure consistent orientation and wiring order; tighten clamps per torque value.
  5. DC wiring: Use PV-rated tinned copper cable, correct cross-section (mm²) for current and length; keep polarity consistent; use MC4 connectors and weatherproof junctions.
  6. Earthing: Bond panel frames and rails to common earth bus with proper conductor size and clamps; install surge protection devices (SPDs) at array and inverter ends.
  7. Testing & commissioning: Measure open-circuit voltages, string currents, insulation resistance; verify inverter settings and meter connections; complete commissioning report.

Solar Cables — Detailed Product Theory

What are Solar Cables? ▼

Overview & purpose

Solar cables are designed to carry DC power from PV modules to combiner boxes, inverters and batteries. They use UV-resistant, weatherproof insulation compounds and tinned copper conductors to resist corrosion and mechanical wear.

Technical specifics

Typical insulation: cross-linked polyethylene (XLPE) or specialized PV compounds rated for UV and temperature extremes. Conductor material: tinned copper. Ratings: voltage rating (eg. 1.5kV in many PV systems), temperature range, UV index, and flame retardant classification.

Types and sizes

  • PV single-core: For long DC runs and trunk cabling.
  • PVC / twin-core DC: For short panel interconnects.
  • Armoured cables: Where mechanical protection is needed.
  • Sizes: 2.5mm²,4mm²,6mm²,10mm²,16mm²,25mm².

Lithium Batteries — Detailed Product Theory

What are Lithium Batteries and why use them? ▼

Overview

Lithium-based batteries (LiFePO4, NMC, LTO etc.) store electrical energy chemically for later use. In solar systems they provide backup power, peak shaving, and time-shifting of renewable generation.

Types & chemistry

  • LiFePO4 (LFP): Excellent cycle life, thermal stability and safety; widely used in residential and commercial storage.
  • NMC / NCA: Higher energy density but slightly less thermal robustness; used where space is constrained.
  • LTO: Exceptional cycle life and fast-charging capability — used in high-duty applications (but costlier).

Inverters — Detailed Product Theory

What is an Inverter? ▼

Overview

The inverter converts DC electricity from panels/batteries into AC electricity and manages MPPT, battery charging and grid synchronization.

Types

  • Grid-Tie: Exports surplus to grid.
  • Off-Grid: Works standalone with batteries.
  • Hybrid: Combines grid + battery.
  • Microinverter: Per-panel inverter for shading resilience.
